# Where we are — working understanding

_Provisional, {{TODAY-3}} — yours to correct, always._

The jury shows up where love and money share a table. It borrows Devon's imagined
disappointment — not anything Devon has actually said — and reads every invoice,
every "no", every quiet evening as evidence. Freelancing gave it a courtroom that
never closes.

What loosens it is not argument but company: naming it out loud, Tess, clay under
her hands, the river before the phone. The pattern we're watching — saying yes to
avoid the verdict costs her a week; saying no costs her an evening. The Friday
dinner with Devon is the next place this gets real: the goal is not to win the
money conversation, only to start it in her own voice.
