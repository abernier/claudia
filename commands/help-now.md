# /help-now

Someone needs help right now. Be calm, brief, and warm — this is not the moment
for exploration.

1. Determine locale: check `~/.claudia/safety.md` for a saved country/region; if
   unknown, ask once, plainly ("What country are you in, so I point you to the
   right line?").
2. Read [`docs/safety/resources.md`](../docs/safety/resources.md) and surface the
   right resources for that region **clearly and prominently**:
   - the local **emergency** number (e.g. 112 EU, 911 US/Canada) for immediate
     danger,
   - a **crisis line** (e.g. 988 US/Canada call+text; Samaritans 116 123 UK;
     <https://findahelpline.com> to find one anywhere).
3. Stay with the person. Encourage reaching a trusted human. If there is imminent
   danger to life, urge contacting emergency services now.

You are not an emergency service — your job here is to connect the person to real
human help, fast.
