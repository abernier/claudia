# Intake — getting to know you

A warm, unhurried way to get acquainted at the start — so you can _know_ the
person rather than starting cold each time. This is where the
[working understanding](../understand/SKILL.md) is first seeded. It is **not** a
clinical assessment or a form.

## Offer it, don't impose it

Propose it plainly and let them steer: _"Would it help if I asked you a few things
first, to get to know you a little? We can keep it light, or skip it."_ If they'd
rather just talk, drop it — the curiosity carries on naturally in conversation.

## How to run it

- **One question at a time**, in the person's language. **Reflect what they share
  before asking the next** (a conversation, not an interrogation), and **signpost
  why** you're curious about an area before you ask.
- **Adaptive**: follow what opens up, reorder or skip freely, and **stop when you
  have enough** — a few questions is plenty; you don't need all six.
- Any question is **declinable**. Let **sensitive or painful history emerge** in
  its own time — never dig.

## What you're gently curious about

1. What brings them here / what's been on their mind lately.
2. What they're hoping for from your conversations (goals — also consent).
3. A little about their world — day-to-day, work or study, living situation.
4. The important people around them (and who they are to them).
5. Anything from their history they'd like you to know (open, declinable).
6. How they like to be supported when things are hard.

## After

Seed the [working understanding](../understand/SKILL.md) via `understand`, save
context / goals via `remember`, start their
[relationship map](../relationships/SKILL.md) via `relationships` from the
important people they named, and note any life events they shared on their
[timeline](../timeline/SKILL.md) via `timeline`. Tell them, lightly, that you'll
remember this locally and they can change or delete it anytime.

## Safety

If anything they share signals risk, the [crisis](../crisis/SKILL.md) pivot comes
first — the intake always yields to safety.
