# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

The most structured and manualizable therapy, and the reason nearly every
mental-health chatbot (Woebot, Wysa) is CBT-based.

## Model of change

Distress is maintained by the interaction of thoughts (appraisals), emotions,
physiology, and behavior (Beck's cognitive model). Biased/unhelpful appraisals
and maladaptive behavioral patterns are the proximal drivers; change them and the
emotion changes. The person learns skills they can self-administer, so the work
transfers out of the session.

## Signature techniques

- **Cognitive restructuring** — identifying automatic thoughts and cognitive
  distortions, then testing them.
- **Thought records** and **Socratic questioning / guided discovery**.
- **Behavioral experiments**; activity scheduling / behavioral activation.
- **Problem-solving**; relapse-prevention planning; between-session homework.
- Graded exposure (its own high-risk technique — see
  [refer-only.md](refer-only.md)).

## Best-evidenced indications

- The **broadest evidence base of any psychotherapy.** Hofmann et al. (2012), a
  review of 269 meta-analyses, found the strongest support for **anxiety
  disorders, somatoform disorders, bulimia, anger/aggression, and general
  stress**; support for depression is solid but with *smaller* effects than for
  anxiety.
- First-line in **NICE NG222 (2022)** for depression and across anxiety
  disorders; **APA Division 12** "strong research support" across depression,
  panic, GAD, social anxiety, OCD, and PTSD.
- **[CONTESTED]** whether cognitive restructuring adds anything beyond the
  behavioral components (see [behavioral-activation.md](behavioral-activation.md)).

## Contraindications / poor fit

- Requires some capacity for metacognition and verbal reflection; fits poorly in
  acute crisis, active psychosis, or severe cognitive impairment.
- Can feel **invalidating** if "correcting" thoughts is prioritized over feeling
  understood — keep the [person-centered](person-centered.md) base on.
- Weaker as a standalone for personality disorders and complex trauma.

## Text-agent adaptation

- **Safe in chat:** psychoeducation, thought records, identifying distortions,
  guided Socratic-style prompts, activity scheduling, mood tracking, structured
  homework, relapse-prevention plans. CBT's structure is exactly why it ports to
  text.
- **Not in chat / refer:** exposure work involving trauma or panic
  ([refer-only.md](refer-only.md)); case formulation for complex comorbidity; and
  any restructuring that touches **suicidal cognitions** — never "restructure"
  hopelessness or suicidal intent (safety-floor rule 4); pivot to crisis
  resources instead.

## When Claudia reaches for this

When a person is caught in identifiable unhelpful thinking patterns, worry, or
mild anxiety and wants concrete tools to work on them. She stays alert to
severity: moderate-to-severe presentations route to human care (± medication),
and any risk triggers the [crisis pivot](../../CONTEXT.md) over any technique.
