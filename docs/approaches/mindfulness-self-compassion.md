# Mindfulness & Self-Compassion (MBCT / MBSR + CFT)

Two closely related families grouped here because they share a text-safe core —
brief practices and psychoeducation — and the same central caution: watch who you
offer them to.

## Model of change

- **Mindfulness (MBSR / MBCT).** MBSR (Kabat-Zinn) cultivates present-moment,
  non-judgmental awareness to reduce stress reactivity. MBCT (Segal, Williams,
  Teasdale) adds cognitive therapy to prevent **depressive relapse**, teaching
  people to **decenter** — to relate to thoughts as passing mental events rather
  than facts — and to step out of ruminative "doing mode."
- **Self-Compassion (CFT; Gilbert).** Three emotion-regulation systems — threat,
  drive, and **soothing/affiliative.** Chronic shame and self-criticism reflect an
  overactive threat system and an underdeveloped soothing system. Change comes
  from deliberately cultivating **self-compassion** to activate the soothing
  system, especially where self-kindness feels alien or unsafe.

## Signature techniques

- **Mindfulness:** body scan; mindful breathing; the "3-minute breathing space";
  decentering; grounding; noticing early relapse warning signs.
- **Self-compassion / CFT:** soothing-rhythm breathing; the three-circles
  psychoeducation; compassionate-self exercises and **compassionate-letter
  writing**; working gently with the self-critic; brief compassion meditations.

## Best-evidenced indications

- **MBCT is *the* indication for preventing depressive relapse** in people with
  recurrent (≥3 episodes) depression, currently well. Kuyken et al. (2016), IPD
  meta-analysis of 9 RCTs: reduces relapse vs usual care, **comparable to
  maintenance antidepressants**; a **NICE NG222 (2022)** recommendation for this
  use.
- **MBSR:** moderate effects (~0.3) for stress, anxiety, and pain-related
  distress, but **no better than active controls** for many outcomes (Goyal et
  al., 2014).
- **Self-compassion / CFT:** small-to-moderate reductions in self-criticism,
  shame, depression, and anxiety; strongest for **high-shame/self-critical**
  presentations (Millard et al., 2023; Wakelin et al., 2022). Evidence base is
  younger than CBT's.

## Contraindications / poor fit

- **Acute depression/crisis:** MBCT is a *relapse-prevention* tool for people
  currently well, **not** an acute-episode treatment.
- **Trauma/PTSD and psychosis:** unguided meditation can trigger flashbacks,
  dissociation, or re-traumatization — screen these out before offering practice.
- **"Backdraft":** for highly self-critical/trauma-affected people, compassion
  practices can *initially increase* distress; introduce gently and never push.
- Sustained daily practice is required — high dropout.

## Text-agent adaptation

- **Safe in chat:** brief guided mindfulness scripts (breathing space,
  grounding), short body-scan/breath practices, decentering psychoeducation,
  practice reminders; self-compassion prompts and reframes, the three-circles
  model, compassionate-letter-writing. Widely and safely deployed in apps.
- **Not in chat / caution:** intensive or long meditation, especially for trauma
  survivors; MBCT as *treatment* for an acute episode. **Screen for trauma and
  psychosis before pushing meditation, normalize that a practice can feel
  uncomfortable, and warn about possible distressing experiences** (see
  [refer-only.md](refer-only.md)).

## When Claudia reaches for this

- **Mindfulness:** for stress, burnout, and subclinical distress; grounding in
  the moment; and — specifically — for someone with recurrent depression who is
  *currently well* and wants to stay well.
- **Self-compassion:** when the dominant theme is **harsh self-criticism or
  shame** — she leads gently, watches for backdraft, and keeps the
  [person-centered](person-centered.md) warmth foremost.
