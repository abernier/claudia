# Behavioral Activation (BA)

Arguably the **single best-fit modality for a text companion**: concrete,
low-interpretation, low-risk, and a natural match for check-ins.

## Model of change

Rooted in operant/respondent learning theory. Depression is maintained by reduced
contact with positive reinforcement and by avoidance/withdrawal that gives
short-term relief but deepens low mood. Change comes from **acting outward** —
re-engaging with valued, rewarding, and mastery activities *regardless of mood* —
breaking the avoidance cycle. Notably makes **no** claim that you must change
thoughts first.

## Signature techniques

- **Activity monitoring and scheduling** (activity–mood tracking).
- **Values / activity hierarchies**; graded task assignment.
- **Functional analysis** of avoidance ("what does this behavior get you / cost
  you?").
- Contingency management; stimulus control; reinforcing small wins.

## Best-evidenced indications

- Strong for **depression, including severe depression.** The **COBRA**
  non-inferiority RCT found BA **non-inferior to full CBT at 12 months**, while
  deliverable by less highly trained staff (Richards et al., 2016).
- Meta-analyses report **medium-to-large effects** for depression (Ekers et al.,
  2014; Cuijpers et al., 2007).
- A **NICE NG222 (2022)** first-line option for depression.

## Contraindications / poor fit

- Physical-health limits on activity.
- Environments genuinely devoid of reinforcers (poverty, abuse) where "do more
  pleasant activities" can ring hollow or **invalidating** — witness the reality
  first.
- Less studied as a primary treatment for anxiety-only presentations.

## Text-agent adaptation

- **Safe in chat:** activity scheduling, mood–activity tracking, values
  checklists, graded task nudges, and reinforcement of small wins. Simple,
  concrete, low-interpretation, and a good match for notifications/check-ins —
  which also add the lightweight "guidance" that most reliably improves digital
  self-help outcomes (Karyotaki et al., 2021).
- **Not in chat / refer:** functional analysis of complex avoidance tied to
  trauma; and when withdrawal reflects acute risk, the [crisis
  pivot](../../CONTEXT.md) comes first — never nudge activity past a disclosure of
  danger.

## When Claudia reaches for this

For mild-to-moderate low mood, inertia, and withdrawal — someone who has stopped
doing the things that used to matter. It is her highest-value, lowest-risk
technique-led move, and it pairs cleanly with the [person-centered](person-centered.md)
base and with [solution-focused](solution-focused.md) small-step goal-setting.
