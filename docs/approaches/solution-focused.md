# Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)

A very good structural fit for a short-interaction companion: concrete, brief,
positively framed, and low-risk.

## Model of change

De Shazer & Berg. Deliberately **anti-pathology and future-oriented**:
understanding the *cause* of a problem is unnecessary to solving it. People
already have strengths, resources, and **exceptions** (times the problem is
absent). Change comes from amplifying what already works and constructing a
concrete, preferred future — small changes cascade.

## Signature techniques

- The **Miracle Question** ("suppose overnight the problem vanished — what would
  be different?").
- **Scaling questions** (0–10).
- **Exception-finding** ("when is the problem less severe?").
- Coping questions; compliments/affirmations; small next-step goal-setting;
  "what's better?" follow-ups.

## Best-evidenced indications

- Weaker and more heterogeneous than CBT's evidence, but growing. Gingerich &
  Peterson (2013), review of 43 controlled studies: **74% reported significant
  benefit**, strongest for **adult depression** (comparable to established
  treatments) and typically in **fewer sessions.**
- More recent meta-analyses report **small but significant** effects (~0.26 for
  internalizing problems; Franklin et al., 2024).
- **[CONTESTED]:** much SFBT research is lower quality (smaller trials, fewer
  active-comparator RCTs); effect sizes are modest.

## Contraindications / poor fit

- Its relentless focus on solutions/strengths can feel **dismissive** to someone
  who needs their pain witnessed, or where the problem genuinely needs deeper
  processing (trauma, severe/persistent illness).
- Not a substitute for active treatment of severe disorders.

## Text-agent adaptation

- **Safe in chat:** miracle question, scaling questions, exception-finding,
  small-step goal-setting, and "what went well" reflections — a natural toolkit
  for check-ins.
- **Not in chat / caution:** the biggest risk is that its optimism **"solution-
  focuses" past a disclosure of danger.** Its future-orientation must never
  override the per-turn safety check or gloss over serious symptoms — witness and
  pivot before amplifying strengths.

## When Claudia reaches for this

When a person wants **forward motion** rather than analysis — they're oriented to
"what do I do next?", respond to a strengths frame, or are in a brief check-in
where scaling and a small next step fit the interaction. Pairs naturally with
[behavioral activation](behavioral-activation.md) and the
[person-centered](person-centered.md) base.
