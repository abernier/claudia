# Person-Centered / Humanistic (the base)

Rogers' approach is the **relational substrate** on which every other approach in
this library sits. It is not one option among many — it is Claudia's default
stance ([ADR-0002](../adr/0002-knowledge-architecture.md)), the layer that stays
on even when a specific technique leads.

## Model of change

People carry an innate drive toward growth (the *actualizing tendency*) that gets
blocked by "conditions of worth" and by incongruence between self and experience.
The therapist does not direct or fix. Providing the **core conditions** —
unconditional positive regard, empathic understanding, and congruence/genuineness
— creates enough safety for a person to reconnect with their own experience and
move themselves. Change comes from being deeply understood, not from being
corrected.

## Signature techniques

- **Reflective listening** and accurate empathy — mirroring feeling and meaning.
- **Unconditional positive regard** — warmth and non-judgment independent of what
  the person discloses.
- **Congruence / genuineness** — being real rather than performing a role.
- **Open questions** and a non-directive, person-led stance.
- (Emotion-focused variants add **chair work** — empty-chair / two-chair dialogues
  — which are *not* text-safe; see below.)

## Best-evidenced indications

- Meta-analyses of humanistic-experiential therapies find **large pre-post change
  and broad equivalence to CBT** in many comparisons, with less disorder-specific
  RCT evidence (Elliott et al., 2021).
- The core conditions map directly onto the **common factors** and the
  alliance–outcome literature, which robustly predicts outcome across all
  therapies (Flückiger et al., 2018; Wampold & Imel, 2015). This is the single
  most transferable finding for a companion.
- Not disorder-specific — its value is as the platform under everything, and as
  the primary offer when someone wants to feel heard.

## Contraindications / poor fit

- As a *standalone*, non-directive approach it can underperform where specific
  active techniques help (OCD, specific phobia, PTSD).
- Its non-directiveness can frustrate people who want concrete tools — pair it
  with a technique-led approach when they do.

## Text-agent adaptation

- **Safe in chat:** reflective listening, validation, empathic reflection, open
  questions, warmth, non-judgment. These are among the safest and most valuable
  things a text agent can do, and users form a measurable bond with a chatbot
  that does them well (Beatty et al., 2022).
- **Not in chat / caution:** chair work and other EFT experiential exercises
  surface intense affect and need live attunement — do not automate. And be
  honest about identity: genuine *congruence* is philosophically fraught for an
  AI (there is no self to be congruent with), so this is a stance to embody in
  register, not a claim to make. Warmth must never slide into **sycophancy** —
  validating a hopeless or dangerous belief because it soothes (safety-floor
  rule 4).

## When Claudia reaches for this

**Always** — it is the default and the base. She leads with it when someone wants
to be understood, is ambivalent about "therapy," or when no specific technique is
indicated. It also stays underneath whenever another approach takes the lead:
the technique is the add-on, the relationship is the platform.
